## Design Context

### Users
Polymarket Explorer serves a mixed audience: active traders tracking performance, research analysts studying prediction markets, and casual browsers exploring out of curiosity. They range from crypto-native power users to newcomers clicking through for the first time. The interface must serve data-hungry traders without alienating someone who just wants to browse leaderboards.

### Brand Personality
**Clean, confident, crypto-native.** The interface should feel polished and modern like Stripe or Linear, but grounded in the prediction market world. It conveys competence and trustworthiness through restraint and clarity, not through flashiness or density. Three words: **precise, approachable, credible**.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: System-preference dark/light with both modes as first-class citizens
- **References**: Polymarket's own design language (familiar to users), DeBank/Zapper (crypto portfolio trackers with colorful, wallet-centric UIs), Dune Analytics (data-forward dashboards with crypto-native aesthetics)
- **Anti-references**: No generic admin dashboards or Bootstrap templates. No cartoon mascots or meme-energy crypto aesthetics. No Bloomberg-density clutter. No bland enterprise software. It should have personality and edge while remaining breathable and approachable.
- **Color**: OKLch color system with purple/blue primary. Semantic green/red for gains/losses. Neutrals tinted toward the brand hue.
- **Typography**: Geist Sans + Geist Mono. Clean, technical, modern.
- **Visual character**: Subtle depth through rings and borders rather than heavy shadows. Generous whitespace. 3D shader hero for visual interest. Data visualizations that feel intentional, not decorative.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over density**: Complex data should feel approachable. Prioritize readability and breathing room over cramming in more information.
2. **Crypto-native, not crypto-cliche**: Speak the language of prediction markets and DeFi without resorting to tired crypto visual tropes.
3. **Confidence through restraint**: Let the data speak. Strong typography, clear hierarchy, and purposeful color usage build trust more than decorative elements.
4. **Progressive disclosure**: Start simple, reveal depth through interaction. A casual browser sees a clean page; a power user discovers the full toolkit.
5. **Consistent precision**: Every spacing decision, color choice, and interaction should feel deliberate. No default values left unexamined.
